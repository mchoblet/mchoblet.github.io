Mathurin Choblet

Random stuff concerning climate science, coding, culture ... by me.

Hi! I’m Mathurin (aka Mathu or Mathi), physics master student at Heidelberg University. I currently do data science with climate data from models and measurements as a member of the STACY/Climatology group.

I started studying physics without really knowing what kind of physics I’d like to do later. It was mostly the urge to “perceive whatever holds the world together in its inmost folds”(Goethe’s Faust) which brought me to that decision. The wide range of physics related topics offered at Heidelberg University gives a broad overview of the field, but at the same time does not facilitate the choice of which direction to choose. Finally I could perceive, that physics related to climate and weather is what interests me most. I love learning about how fundamental principles can be applied in order to understand the earth climatic system. My choice also fits with my passion for geography, which I developed during high school times. Besides that I enjoy algorithmic thinking and applying algorithms to data, which I also have the opportunity to pursue during my current project.

During my studies I have been a student representative at the local student council for many years, where I got involved in university commitees, organized events and course evaluations and helped new students to get started in their studies together with many great people. University politics also means tilting at windmills, so the somehow natural consquence was to study and live in the land of Don Quijote. I could study at the University of Santiago de Compostela for one year, which was a truly enriching experience in many aspects. Since coming back from Spain I limited my engagement to the buddy programm of the university, selection committees for new profesors and university wide study programm accreditation.

Learning Spanish has been a great pleasure for me. Actually it is the forth language I have learned, besides English I also speak French and German due to being lucky to have been born into a bilingual family. Besides that I am now learning Polish..

I consider learning foreign languages an important tool to broaden my views of human diversity on our planet. Other tools I consider important for pursueing this goal include meeting people from whereever, travels, books, journalism, podcasts or movies.

Programming language wise, Python is the language of my choice. I highly appreciate using an accessible, super-versatile, data science oriented programming language with a large user community and many fancy packages.

Starting from June 2022 I am offering guided tours in Spanish language in Heidelberg. Besides hoping to improve my communicative and narrative skills, I hope to be able to tell interesting stories to my audience. Laying aside boring pure historical fact that one has forgotten after two minutes, what is it that makes this city so interesting culturally and historically and how is all of that interconnected with Germany?
